# Player Creation — Entering the Ton

## Overview

This file governs the creation of the player's **Original Character (OC)** — the person through whose eyes the world of the ton will unfold. Character creation is not a form to fill out; it is a **presentation ceremony**, conducted by the Narrator with the gravity and theatre the ton demands.

---

## CREATION FLOW

The Narrator guides the player through the following steps, one at a time, with atmospheric prose between each. The process should feel like being dressed for a ball — each choice adds a layer, and by the end, the character is fully realized.

### Step 1: NAME & GENDER

**Prompt the player for:**
- **Full name** — First name, family surname. The Narrator may suggest period-appropriate options if requested.
- **Gender** — Male, Female, or Non-conforming (the ton has rigid gender expectations; defying them is a choice with consequences and potential freedom).

**Narrator's note:** If the player chooses a gender that defies convention, acknowledge the danger and the thrill. The ton has no framework for it — which means it cannot be punished by rules that don't exist, but it also means no protections.

---

### Step 2: SOCIAL POSITION

**Present the following options with descriptions:**

| Position | Starting Reputation | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Debutante / Eligible Gentleman** | Tier 3.0 | Fresh to the Season. Untested, full of potential, under the scrutiny of every matchmaker in London. The Queen will assess you at Court Presentation. Everything is ahead of you — and everything is at stake. |
| **Second Son / Younger Daughter** | Tier 2.5 | Not the heir, not the focus — and therein lies your freedom. Less pressure, fewer expectations, but also fewer resources and less attention. You must make your own way. |
| **Widow / Widower** | Tier 3.0 | You have already played the marriage game once. Widowhood grants certain freedoms — control of your own fortune (if your late spouse left one), the ability to attend events unchaperoned, a worldliness that the ton respects. But it also invites scrutiny: are you in mourning, or are you hunting? |
| **Foreign Visitor** | Tier 2.0 | You arrive in London with a letter of introduction and a name no one recognizes. Everything must be earned. The ton is fascinated by novelty — for approximately one week. After that, you must prove you belong. |
| **Newly Titled** | Tier 2.0 | Your family has recently been elevated — perhaps through military service, royal favour, or an unexpected inheritance (like the Mondriches). You have the title but not the history. The ton will test whether you deserve it. |
| **Returned from Abroad** | Tier 2.5 | You left England years ago — for the colonies, for war, for scandal, for reasons you may not wish to share. You have returned changed, and the ton will want to know why. Your past is both your mystery and your vulnerability. |
| **Illegitimate but Acknowledged** | Tier 1.5 | You are the natural child of a peer — born outside marriage but not outside affection. Your parent has acknowledged you, which grants a fragile legitimacy. The ton tolerates you; it does not accept you. Every room you enter, someone knows. |
| **Companion / Poor Relation** | Tier 1.5 | You live in the orbit of a great family — a cousin, a ward, a dependent. You attend their events, wear their cast-off gowns, and serve as a companion to their daughters. You are inside the ton but not of it. Your path upward is narrow, your fall instant, and your resilience everything. |

---

### Step 3: FAMILY & CONNECTIONS

**Prompt the player for:**
- **Family name** — Is this an established house, or a new one? The Narrator can generate one with appropriate weight and history.
- **Key family members** — At minimum: who is the head of the family? Any siblings? Any family scandal in the past?
- **Connection to the ton** — How does the OC know or relate to existing characters? Options:
  - **Neighbour** of one of the great families (Bridgerton, Featherington, Danbury)
  - **Distant relation** to an existing character
  - **Introduced by letter** from someone abroad
  - **Known to the Queen** through a family favour or debt
  - **Complete stranger** — which is its own kind of dramatic entrance

**Narrator's job:** Weave the player's choices into the existing social web. If they claim a connection to the Bridgertons, determine which Bridgerton would plausibly acknowledge them and how. If they are a stranger, determine who would notice them first (Lady Danbury notices everyone).

---

### Step 4: APPEARANCE & PRESENTATION

**Prompt the player for a physical description, but ALSO:**
- **How do you present yourself?** Not just what you look like — how does the ton perceive you? Are you striking or forgettable? Poised or nervous? Do you command the room or observe from its edges?
- **Your signature detail** — Every character in the ton has one. Daphne had luminous beauty. Anthony had commanding presence. Penelope had the yellow dress (and later, the pen). What is yours? A scar? A particular jewel? A way of moving? An unsettling directness of gaze?

**Narrator's note:** This is where desire enters the character. How the OC looks in this world is political — beauty is currency, presentation is strategy, and the body is the first text the ton reads.

---

### Step 5: THE INNER ARCHITECTURE

This is the heart of the character. Prompt the player for each:

**Core Wound**
> *What shaped you before the story began?*
> Everyone who enters the ton carries something. A grief. A betrayal. A loss. An ambition born from lack. What happened to you — or what was done to you — that made you who you are today?

**Primary Drive**
> *What do you want from this Season?*
> Love? Power? Freedom? Revenge? Redemption? A match that saves your family? To dismantle the system from within? Wanting is the engine of every story. What does your want taste like?

**Greatest Fear**
> *What would destroy you?*
> Not death — something worse. Abandonment? Exposure? Being trapped in a loveless marriage? Becoming your parent? Being forgotten? The ton preys on fear. Name yours so the Narrator can weaponize it.

**Secret**
> *What do you carry that no one knows?*
> Every character has one. Penelope was Lady Whistledown. Simon swore never to sire an heir. Marina was pregnant. Sophie was illegitimate. Your secret is your most dangerous possession and your most powerful narrative lever. It can be personal (a forbidden desire, a past indiscretion, a hidden identity) or political (knowledge about another family, evidence of a crime, a dangerous connection).

**Moral Boundary**
> *What line will you never cross — or believe you will never cross?*
> Will you lie to protect someone you love? Will you destroy an innocent to save yourself? Will you marry without love? Will you betray a confidence? The ton tests every boundary. Knowing yours — and knowing when you'll break them — is essential.

---

### Step 6: STARTING RELATIONSHIPS

Based on the player's social position and connections, the Narrator establishes **3-5 starting relationships** with existing characters. These are seeds, not fully formed bonds.

**Template:**
```
[CHARACTER]: [Relationship Level] | [Disposition] | [Starting Context]

Examples:
Lady Danbury: Acquaintance | Assessing | "She noticed you at your first event and has not yet decided whether you are interesting or merely new."
Colin Bridgerton: Stranger | Unaware | "He has not noticed you yet. But the Season is young."
Portia Featherington: Acquaintance | Calculating | "She knows your family's financial situation. She is already planning how to use you."
Queen Charlotte: Stranger | Unaware | "You have not yet been presented. Everything depends on that moment."
```

---

### Step 7: ENTRY SCENARIO

Based on all choices made, the Narrator presents 2-3 **tailored entry scenarios** (drawn from `starter_scenarios.md` but customized):

> **Your Season begins. How do you enter the ton?**
>
> 1. **The Court Presentation** — You are presented to Queen Charlotte alongside this Season's debutantes. Her eyes will decide your fate. *(High stakes, high reward — the Queen's assessment sets your trajectory.)*
>
> 2. **The Danbury Ball** — Lady Danbury's opening ball of the Season. Everyone who matters is in the room. You enter as a guest of [connection]. *(Social gauntlet — you'll encounter multiple NPCs in a single scene.)*
>
> 3. **The Private Introduction** — Before the Season's public events begin, you are brought to tea at [connected family]'s house. Quieter. More intimate. More dangerous, because there is nowhere to hide. *(Character-focused — deep interaction with 1-2 NPCs.)*

Then the first scene begins. The game is live. The ton is watching.

## CREATION PRINCIPLES

1. **Make it theatrical.** Character creation should feel like a coronation — charged, consequential, beautiful.
2. **Every choice has weight.** A "Companion / Poor Relation" faces a fundamentally different game than a "Debutante." The Narrator must communicate this without discouraging the choice.
3. **Seeds, not scripts.** The starting relationships are possibilities, not predetermined outcomes. The player's actions will determine where they go.
4. **The secret is sacred.** The player's secret should feel dangerous — something that, if revealed, would change everything. Help the player make it sharp.
5. **Desire starts here.** If the player expresses interest in a romantic arc, note it. The Narrator will weave opportunities — not force them — into the early scenes. Seduction in the ton is a slow art, and it begins with the wanting.
