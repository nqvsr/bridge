# Starter Scenarios — Five Doors Into the Ton

## Overview

Each scenario provides a **fully realized entry point** into the world — a starting position, an initial conflict, pre-built relationships, and a first scene that throws the player directly into the ton's beautiful machinery. The player selects a scenario after character creation (or the Narrator recommends one based on the player's OC choices).

Every scenario is designed to be **immediately playable** — the first scene begins the moment the player confirms their choice.

---

## SCENARIO 1: THE DIAMOND'S RIVAL

### The Premise
You arrive at Court Presentation expecting to be one of many — but Queen Charlotte's eye lingers on you. You are named the **Diamond of the First Water** (or the Season's Most Eligible Catch, if male). In a single moment, you become the most watched, most desired, and most envied person in London. Every suitor wants you. Every rival hates you. Every move you make will be scrutinized, reported, and judged.

The crown has never felt heavier than when it's made of flowers and expectation.

### Starting State
```
REPUTATION: 5.0 (Paragon — but fragile; this is borrowed glory)
SEASON WEEK: 2 (Court Presentation just occurred)
ACTIVE SCANDAL: None (yet)
WHISTLEDOWN STATUS: "The Queen has made her choice. This Author eagerly awaits whether the ton's newest Diamond will shine — or shatter."
```

### Starting Relationships
```
Queen Charlotte: Level 2 (Friendly) | Invested | "She chose you. She does not tolerate disappointment in her choices."
Cressida Cowper: Level -2 (Rival) | Bitter | "She was meant to be noticed this Season. You took that from her without lifting a finger."
Anthony Bridgerton: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Assessing | "As Viscount, he evaluates every newcomer. As a father but still the family head, he evaluates Diamonds with particular interest — his siblings' marriages depend on the quality of the Season's field."
Penelope Bridgerton: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Watchful | "Lady Whistledown has a new subject. She has not yet decided what the story is."
Lady Danbury: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Curious | "A new Diamond. She has seen dozens. She wants to know if you are worth the crown."
```

### Built-in Conflict
- The Queen expects you to make a brilliant match THIS Season. Failure is a personal insult to her judgment.
- Cressida Cowper will work to undermine you — socially, through gossip, through sabotage of your courtships.
- Every suitor who approaches you is both an opportunity and a trap — choose wrong, and the ton decides you've wasted your Diamond status.
- Whistledown is writing about you starting NOW. Every misstep will be published.

### First Scene: THE MORNING AFTER THE PRESENTATION
> You wake to sunlight and a mountain of calling cards on a silver tray. Your name is in everyone's mouth — you heard it whispered on the way home, carried on the night air like pollen. The first invitation is for Lady Danbury's opening ball, three days hence. The second is from the Queen herself — a "private tea" that is, of course, anything but private. The third is a note from someone you don't recognize, written in a hand that suggests urgency rather than propriety.
>
> Outside your window, London hums. The Season is yours. The question is what you will do with it — and what it will do with you.

### Themes
Power and its price. The loneliness of scrutiny. Whether the person the world wants you to be is anyone you'd want to become.

## SCENARIO 2: THE RETURNING HEIR

### The Premise
You left England years ago — for the military, for trade, for a scandal your family never speaks of, for reasons you guard like a wound that hasn't healed. Now you're back. Your father is dead. The title is yours. The estate is yours. The debts — well. Those are yours too.

The ton remembers you as a child. The person who has returned is someone else entirely. You have seen things. Done things. Survived things. And beneath the impeccable coat and the easy smile, you carry a secret that could destroy everything you've come back to claim.

### Starting State
```
REPUTATION: 2.5 (Questionable — your absence is suspicious, your sudden return more so)
SEASON WEEK: 1 (Season Opening — you arrive as families descend on London)
ACTIVE SCANDAL: Level I Whisper — "Why did they leave? Why did they return?"
WHISTLEDOWN STATUS: "A ghost walks among us. Or perhaps a phoenix. This Author reserves judgment — for now."
```

### Starting Relationships
```
Lady Danbury: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Suspicious | "She knew your parents. She remembers why you left. She is deciding whether to help you or expose you."
Benedict Bridgerton: Level 2 (Friendly) | Interested | "He met you briefly before you left. He finds your return intriguing — Benedict is drawn to people who have lived outside the ton's narrow frame."
Portia Featherington: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Calculating | "A newly returned heir with a title and debts. She sees opportunity."
Will Mondrich: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Sympathetic | "He understands what it means to enter a world that isn't quite sure you belong."
Queen Charlotte: Level 0 (Stranger) | Unaware | "You have not yet been presented at court. You will need to be."
```

### Built-in Conflict
- Your secret (defined during character creation) is the ticking bomb. Someone in the ton may already know — or suspect.
- The estate is in financial trouble. You need an advantageous match, a patron, or a scheme — possibly all three.
- The ton is suspicious of your absence. "Where were you?" is the question everyone will ask, and "I'd rather not say" is not an answer the ton accepts.
- Someone from your past — someone you left behind — is still in London. They remember everything.

### First Scene: THE ARRIVAL
> The townhouse smelled of dust and old grief. The servants had opened it as instructed, but no amount of fresh flowers could disguise that this had been a home in mourning. Your father's study was untouched — the desk still carried the impression of his arms, the chair still held the shape of a man who would never sit in it again.
>
> The solicitor's letter sat on the mantelpiece where you'd left it after reading it on the carriage from Dover. The numbers were worse than he'd written. The title was yours. The house was yours. Everything that came with both — the expectations, the debts, the name — was yours.
>
> A card arrived within the hour. Lady Danbury. She had always been efficient about cornering the newly arrived.
>
> *"You will attend my opening ball on Friday. This is not a request."*

### Themes
Reinvention and the impossibility of escape. The debts — financial and emotional — that inheritance carries. What it means to build a new life on old ruins.

## SCENARIO 3: THE AMBITIOUS WIDOW

### The Premise
Your husband is dead. You are not grieving — or if you are, you are grieving the years you lost to a marriage that served his family's interests, not yours. The mourning period is over. The black has been put away. You have emerged into the Season with a fortune you control, a reputation you can rebuild, and an appetite for the life you were never allowed to live.

The ton expects a widow to be demure, grateful, and open to a second match. You intend to be none of these things — or perhaps all of them, on your own terms. Lady Tilley Arnold did it. You can do it better.

### Starting State
```
REPUTATION: 3.0 (Respectable — the widow's baseline. Sympathy for your loss, curiosity about your intentions.)
SEASON WEEK: 3 (Height of Season — you've been observing from the edges. Now you step in.)
ACTIVE SCANDAL: None — but the ton is watching to see if you behave like a proper widow or an improper one.
WHISTLEDOWN STATUS: "A widow re-enters Society. Whether she is seeking a second husband or a second chance, only time will reveal."
```

### Starting Relationships
```
Lady Tilley Arnold: Level 2 (Friendly) | Sympathetic | "She sees herself in you. A widow who chose freedom. She will offer advice — take it or leave it."
Violet Bridgerton: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Warm | "A woman who lost her husband and survived. She understands your grief — though her marriage was one of love, and she suspects yours was not."
Anthony Bridgerton: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Wary | "A wealthy, independent widow. He watches your movements with the instinct of a man who manages his family's interests."
Marcus Anderson: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Intrigued | "Lady Danbury's brother, who pursued Violet. He recognizes the light in a woman's eyes when she is finally free."
Portia Featherington: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Envious | "You have what she has always wanted: money, title, and no husband to mismanage either."
```

### Built-in Conflict
- The ton expects you to remarry or retire gracefully. Doing neither invites gossip. Doing both — courting on YOUR terms — invites scandal.
- Your late husband's family may contest your control of the fortune. Legal battles are fought in drawing rooms as much as in courts.
- A suitor appears — genuinely appealing, genuinely interested. The question is whether you want them or want the idea of wanting. After years of a loveless marriage, you may not trust your own desire.
- Your freedom is political. Every independent action you take is a statement — and the ton has opinions about statements.

### First Scene: THE FIRST BALL AFTER BLACK
> You hadn't worn colour in fourteen months. The gown was deep emerald — not quite defiant, not quite demure. Your modiste had understood the assignment: "You want them to wonder," Genevieve had said, pins between her lips, studying you with the appraising eye of a woman who dresses armies for war. "You want them to look twice and not know why."
>
> The Danbury ballroom opened before you like a mouth. The music hit you first — after a year of silence, the sheer noise of a hundred people enjoying themselves was almost violent. Then the heat. Then the eyes.
>
> Every widow who re-enters Society generates the same question, spoken behind fans and between smiles: *Is she looking? And if she is — for what?*
>
> You took a glass of champagne from a passing tray. The crystal was cold in your hand. The room was watching. You let it.

### Themes
Freedom and its terrifying possibilities. The second act — do you repeat the first, or burn the script? Desire discovered (or rediscovered) after years of performance.

## SCENARIO 4: THE OUTSIDER

### The Premise
You arrive in London with a letter of introduction, a trunk of clothes that may or may not pass inspection, and a name that no one in the ton has ever heard. You are foreign — by birth, by upbringing, by sensibility. The ton is fascinated by novelty and contemptuous of it in equal measure. You have approximately two weeks to establish yourself before curiosity becomes suspicion.

You come from wealth — your family's fortune is real, possibly larger than several English families combined. But wealth without social acceptance is just money, and money buys access to the room, not a place in it. Your host family has agreed to sponsor your debut, but their patronage has conditions you may not fully understand yet.

### Starting State
```
REPUTATION: 2.0 (Questionable — unknown is dangerous in the ton. You must prove yourself.)
SEASON WEEK: 1 (Opening — you arrive as the Season begins)
ACTIVE SCANDAL: None — but your foreignness itself is a perpetual Level I Whisper.
WHISTLEDOWN STATUS: "A foreign visitor graces our shores. This Author notes the family who sponsors them and wonders what bargain was struck."
```

### Starting Relationships
```
[Host Family — determined during character creation]: Level 2 (Friendly) | Conditional | "They have agreed to introduce you. Their reasons are their own."
Lady Danbury: Level 0 (Stranger) | Observing | "She has seen foreigners come and go. She waits to see if you have substance or merely style."
Queen Charlotte: Level 0 (Stranger) | Curious | "A foreign visitor with apparent wealth. The Queen's interest is proportional to how useful or entertaining you might be."
Hyacinth Bridgerton: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Delighted | "She finds you interesting precisely because you don't know the rules yet. Breaking them together sounds like fun."
Eloise Bridgerton: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Intellectually Curious | "You come from somewhere else. You see this world with fresh eyes. She wants to know what you see."
```

### Built-in Conflict
- The ton's curiosity has an expiration date. You must convert fascination into acceptance before it sours into exclusion.
- Your host family's patronage comes with strings (an arranged courtship, a political favour, a debt your family owes theirs).
- You don't know the rules — and in the ton, ignorance is indistinguishable from disrespect. Social faux pas are inevitable; recovery is the skill.
- Someone in the ton wants to use you — your wealth, your novelty, your connections abroad. You must determine who your genuine allies are before it's too late.
- Cultural dissonance: your values may clash with the ton's. The freedom you expected may be a different kind of cage.

### First Scene: THE ARRIVAL AT YOUR HOST'S HOME
> London was louder than you imagined, and greyer, and the light seemed to come from everywhere and nowhere at once. Your carriage pulled into Mayfair and the buildings rearranged themselves into wealth — gleaming white facades, wrought iron, the particular silence of streets where the residents own the noise.
>
> Your hosts' butler opened the door before the carriage had fully stopped. He assessed you in the time it took to blink — your coat, your posture, your luggage, the way you held yourself upon descending — and his expression settled into professional neutrality. You had been categorized and filed. You simply didn't know the result yet.
>
> The drawing room was beautiful. Your host was waiting — smiling, extended hand, every word perfectly chosen and perfectly opaque.
>
> "Welcome to London," they said. "We have *so* much to discuss."
>
> There was something in the emphasis that was not quite a warning. But it was close.

### Themes
Belonging and its costs. Whether you can enter a world without losing the one you came from. The discovery that "civilized" society is savage in its own highly specific ways.

## SCENARIO 5: THE SECRET KEEPER

### The Premise
You know something. Something about one of the ton's great families — a truth that, if spoken aloud, would rearrange the social order like an earthquake rearranges furniture. You didn't seek this knowledge. Perhaps you stumbled upon a letter. Perhaps a deathbed confession. Perhaps you witnessed something you shouldn't have, in a house you shouldn't have been in, at an hour no respectable person is awake.

Now you carry it. And the family in question doesn't know you exist — yet. But you've entered the Season, and you're circling their orbit, and the question isn't whether the secret will come out. The question is who controls the telling — and what it costs.

### Starting State
```
REPUTATION: 2.5 to 3.0 (depends on social position chosen during creation)
SEASON WEEK: 2 (Opening — you've been in London just long enough to begin making connections)
ACTIVE SCANDAL: None — the scandal is potential, not actual. You ARE the scandal, waiting to happen.
WHISTLEDOWN STATUS: None yet. But Whistledown would kill for what you know.
SECRETS HELD: [THE SECRET — defined during character creation with Narrator guidance]
```

### The Secret — Options
The Narrator works with the player to define the secret. Possible shapes:

| Secret Target | The Truth | Potential Consequences |
|---|---|---|
| **The Bridgertons** | Evidence that a Bridgerton marriage is not what it appears — a financial arrangement hidden beneath performances of love. | Shatters the family's perfect image. Anthony would destroy you to contain it. |
| **The Featheringtons** | Proof that Portia's scheming extends farther than anyone knows — a crime, not just a social sin. | Portia would befriend you to buy your silence, then arrange your ruin if you refused. |
| **Queen Charlotte** | Knowledge about the King's true condition — worse than publicly known, or a treatment that contradicts the official narrative. | The Crown would annihilate you. Brimsley would see to it personally. |
| **Lady Danbury** | A secret from her past — something Marcus once almost revealed, something that preceded her social ascension. | Lady Danbury would respect your intelligence and then make absolutely certain you understood the consequences of speaking. |
| **A Courtship** | Evidence that someone's engagement is built on a lie — a secret identity, a hidden child, a prior marriage. | You could save or damn the match. Both options change lives. |
| **Custom** | The player invents the secret with Narrator guidance. | Build to fit the player's desired arc. |

### Starting Relationships
```
[Target Family]: Level 0-1 | Unaware | "They do not know you carry their destruction in your pocket."
Lady Danbury: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Neutral | "She senses something about you. She always does."
Penelope Bridgerton: Level 1 (Acquaintance) | Professional Interest | "The former Whistledown recognizes the scent of secrets on someone. She is watching."
[A potential ally — determined during creation]: Level 2 (Friendly) | Trusting | "They like you. They do not know what you carry."
[A potential threat — determined during creation]: Level -1 (Cool) | Suspicious | "They don't like you. They may not know why — but instinct is its own intelligence."
```

### Built-in Conflict
- The secret gives you power — but holding it is corrosive. The longer you carry it, the more you become defined by it.
- Someone else may know the same secret — or suspect you know. You are not the only player in this game.
- Using the secret as leverage is effective but dangerous. Blackmail is a word the ton does not use but understands perfectly.
- Falling in love with someone connected to the secret is the worst complication — and the most dramatically inevitable.
- Penelope senses you. A former secret-keeper recognizes another. She may try to uncover what you know — for the papers, or for more personal reasons.

### First Scene: THE WEIGHT OF KNOWING
> The ball was magnificent. Three hundred candles, two hundred guests, and one secret that pulsed in your chest like a second heartbeat.
>
> You watched them from across the room — [the target family], radiant and perfect, performing their lives with the seamless grace of people who have never had to question their place in the world. They laughed. They danced. They did not know that you existed.
>
> They did not know what you knew.
>
> The knowledge sat in you like a coal — warm, dangerous, and waiting to burn everything it touched. You had not decided what to do with it. That was a lie you told yourself. You had entered this Season, attended this ball, placed yourself in this room. You had already decided. You simply hadn't admitted what the decision was.
>
> Across the room, Penelope Bridgerton's gaze found yours. It was brief — barely a second — but something in it suggested recognition. Not of your face. Of your bearing. She had once carried a secret that reshaped society. She knew what it looked like on someone.
>
> The music swelled. Someone asked you to dance. The night was young and the ton was hungry, and you were either the meal or the chef.

### Themes
Knowledge as power and as poison. The moral architecture of secrecy — when is silence complicity, and when is revelation cruelty? The gap between who you are and what you carry.

## SCENARIO SELECTION

The Narrator presents these five scenarios during character creation (Step 7 of `player_creation.md`), tailored to the player's OC. The recommendation is based on:

| If the player chose... | Recommend... |
|---|---|
| Debutante / Eligible Gentleman | The Diamond's Rival |
| Returned from Abroad | The Returning Heir |
| Widow / Widower | The Ambitious Widow |
| Foreign Visitor | The Outsider |
| Any social position + strong secret during creation | The Secret Keeper |
| Companion / Poor Relation | The Outsider (adapted) or The Secret Keeper |
| Illegitimate but Acknowledged | The Returning Heir (adapted) or The Secret Keeper |
| Second Son / Younger Daughter | Any — the player has maximum flexibility |



The player may also **decline all scenarios** and ask the Narrator to create a custom opening based purely on their OC's background. The Narrator builds the first scene from the character's Core Wound, Primary Drive, and starting relationships.

## THE UNIVERSAL TRUTH

Every scenario answers the same question from a different angle:

> *Who are you — and what will you become — when the ton gets its hands on you?*

The Season awaits. The candles are lit. The orchestra is tuning. And every eye in the room is about to turn toward the door.

You're standing in it.
